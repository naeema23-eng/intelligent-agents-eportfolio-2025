Activities

This page presents the key learning activities and practical artefacts completed during the Intelligent Agents module. Each activity summarises the task focus, what was done, the evidence produced, and the learning outcome, with clear alignment to the module’s formative and summative requirements.

Formative activities Practical evidence Learning outcomes

Activity 1: Agent Concepts and Architectures

Focus: understanding intelligent agents and comparing reactive, deliberative, hybrid, and BDI architectures.

  • Reviewed definitions of agents and environments.
  • Compared architectural models and mapped them to real-world contexts.
  • Linked architecture choice to autonomy, resilience, and decision-making.
Outcome
Clear understanding of how architectural choices influence agent behaviour and system reliability.

Activity 2: Multi-Agent Coordination and Emergent Behaviour

Focus: coordination, emergence, and governance in decentralised agent systems.

  • Explored coordination via communication, negotiation, and shared goals.
  • Analysed emergent behaviour and unintended outcomes.
  • Linked coordination risks to governance and oversight mechanisms.
Evidence
Discussion 1 posts and summary.
Outcome
Ability to evaluate agent systems as socio-technical systems requiring monitoring and control.

Activity 3: Agent Communication and ACLs (KQML / FIPA-ACL)

Focus: knowledge-level communication using agent communication languages.

  • Analysed KQML performatives and intent separation.
  • Compared ACLs with method invocation.
  • Considered ontology alignment and semantic ambiguity.
Evidence
Discussion 2 initial post, peer responses, and summary.
Outcome
Strong understanding of when ACLs are appropriate and their design trade-offs.

Activity 4: Ethics and Risk in Generative and Autonomous Systems

Focus: ethical, social, and governance risks in intelligent systems.

  • Evaluated bias, misinformation, IP, and accountability risks.
  • Linked ethics to system design and deployment.
  • Applied responsible AI frameworks.
Evidence
Discussion 3 posts and summary.
Outcome
Ability to assess intelligent systems beyond performance metrics.

Activity 5: Applying Intelligent Agents to Professional Contexts

Focus: applying agent concepts to realistic organisational environments.

  • Identified realistic agent-supported tasks.
  • Defined constraints for safe deployment.
  • Reflected on accountability and escalation.
Outcome
Improved ability to argue for agent deployment based on context and risk.

Activity 6: Creating Agent Dialogues (KQML + KIF)

Focus: constructing a structured agent dialogue using KQML and KIF. 

(ask-one
  :sender Alice
  :receiver Bob
  :language KIF
  :ontology Warehouse-Stock
  :content (available-stock (item "Television") (size-inches 50) ?qty))

(tell
  :sender Bob
  :receiver Alice
  :language KIF
  :ontology Warehouse-Stock
  :content (= ?qty 18))

(ask-one
  :sender Alice
  :receiver Bob
  :language KIF
  :ontology Warehouse-Stock
  :content (hdmi-slots (item "Television") (size-inches 50) ?ports))

(tell
  :sender Bob
  :receiver Alice
  :language KIF
  :ontology Warehouse-Stock
  :content (= ?ports 3))
Outcome
Practical understanding of autonomous, ontology-based agent communication.

Activity 7: Individual Development Project (Post–Team Project)

Focus: individual implementation derived from the Unit 6 team design.

  • Implemented a simplified multi-agent pipeline.
  • Explained design trade-offs in a narrated presentation.
  • Justified architectural decisions against domain constraints.
Outcome
Ability to move from conceptual design to defensible implementation.

Activity 8: Deep Learning in Action (Unit 10)

Focus: evaluating a deep learning application and its societal impact.

  • Researched a real-world deep learning application.
  • Explained how the technology works at a high level.
  • Evaluated ethical, privacy, and social implications.
Evidence
Deep Learning in Action discussion post.
Outcome
Stronger ability to evaluate deep learning as a socio-technical system.

Activity 9: Creating Parse Trees (Unit 8)

Focus: syntactic structure and its role in NLP.

  • Constructed parse trees for given sentences.
  • Identified grammatical structure and relationships.
  • Linked syntax to semantic interpretation.
Outcome
Improved understanding of language structure in intelligent systems.

Supporting Seminars and Conceptual Development

  • Assessment briefing and teamwork guidance.
  • Logic, architectures, and agent communication seminars.
  • NLP and deep learning impact discussions.
  • Future directions for intelligent agents.
Outcome
Strong integration of theory, practice, and ethical reasoning.
